Why Entrepreneurs Need Long-term Purpose

As an entrepreneur, it’s easy to get caught up in your current projects and business objectives. However, focusing solely on immediate goals can lead to burnout and a lack of direction once those goals are achieved. Just like Olympic athletes who often face depression after reaching their pinnacle, entrepreneurs can feel lost without a broader purpose. This is where the concept of “ikigai” comes into play—a Japanese term meaning “a reason for being.” Finding your ikigai can provide a deeper sense of purpose and drive, beyond just your business.

Why You Need Goals Beyond Business

The Olympic Syndrome

Many Olympic athletes, including Michael Phelps, have experienced depression after achieving their ultimate goals. The same can happen to entrepreneurs who define their success solely by their business achievements. Once you reach that goal, you might find yourself asking, “Now what?” Without a broader purpose, the joy of success can quickly turn into a feeling of emptiness.

The Importance of Ikigai

Ikigai combines passion, mission, vocation, and profession. It’s about finding what you love, what the world needs, what you can be paid for, and what you’re good at. This holistic approach ensures that your life’s purpose isn’t limited to just one aspect, such as your business.

Scientific Support

Research shows that having a sense of purpose can lead to a longer, healthier life. A study published in the Journal of Psychological Science found that individuals with a higher sense of purpose had a 15% lower risk of death compared to those without one . Another study from the University of Michigan found that purposeful individuals had lower levels of inflammatory markers, suggesting better health outcomes .

How to Find Your Purpose

Self-Reflection

Start with introspection. Ask yourself what truly makes you happy and fulfilled. What activities make you lose track of time? These are often indicators of your passions.

Seek Feedback

Talk to people who know you well. They can provide valuable insights into your strengths and potential areas of interest that you might overlook.

Experiment

Try new things. Volunteer, take up a new hobby, or join a group that aligns with your interests. This can help you discover new passions and broaden your perspective.

Applying Ikigai to Business

Align Your Business with Your Values

Ensure that your business reflects your personal values and passions. This alignment can create a more fulfilling work environment and drive long-term success.

Diversify Your Goals

Set goals that go beyond financial success. Focus on personal growth, community impact, and innovation. These broader goals can provide a sense of accomplishment and keep you motivated.

Build a Supportive Culture

Foster a company culture that encourages personal development and work-life balance. This can help you and your employees find greater satisfaction and purpose in your work.

Real-life Examples

Elon Musk

Elon Musk’s vision extends far beyond his companies. His ultimate goal is to make humanity a multi-planetary species. This grand vision keeps him motivated and drives his various ventures, from SpaceX to Tesla.

Patagonia

Patagonia’s mission goes beyond selling outdoor gear. The company is committed to environmental sustainability and activism. This broader purpose resonates with its customers and employees, creating a loyal community and a strong brand.

Finding a purpose beyond your immediate business goals is crucial for long-term success and fulfillment. By discovering your ikigai and integrating it into your life and business, you can avoid burnout, stay motivated, and achieve a greater sense of satisfaction.
