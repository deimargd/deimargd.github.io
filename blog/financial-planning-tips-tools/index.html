Financial planning may be intimidating. It might be difficult to know where to begin with student loan debt, increased living costs, and low starting incomes.


Building a solid financial foundation, on the other hand, is critical for long-term financial success.

How to build a strong financial foundation


1. Create a budget


Those who form and stick to a budget are more likely to have higher levels of savings and lower levels of debt, according to the National Endowment for Financial Education.

A budget allows you to keep track of your income and spending so you can see where your money is going. Begin by documenting your monthly spending and categorizing them into fixed expenses (e.g., rent, utilities) and variable expenses (e.g., groceries, entertainment). You can establish a budget that corresponds with your financial goals if you have a clear picture of your expenditures.

The “50/30/20 rule” is used to implement a budget, which allocates 50% of income to essential expenses, 30% to discretionary expenses, and 20% to savings and debt repayment.

2. Save for emergencies


According to Federal Reserve research, roughly 40% of Americans would struggle to handle a $400 unexpected bill.

Aim to save at least three to six months’ worth of living expenses in a high-yield savings account. This will offer you peace of mind that you can deal with any unforeseen bills that arise.

3. Pay off high-interest debt


Credit card debt, for example, may quickly mount and become a financial hardship. Financial professionals have advocated and found the debt avalanche strategy to be beneficial in debt reduction. Pay off the highest-interest debt first, then work your way down the list. To save money on interest, consider shifting high-interest debt to a lower-interest-rate account.

4. Invest for retirement


It is never too soon to begin saving for retirement. Take advantage of your employer’s 401(k) plan and contribute as much as you can afford. If not, try starting an individual retirement account (IRA) and making monthly contributions. According to Vanguard research, a well-diversified investment portfolio can give greater returns while lowering risk.

5. Seek professional advice


According to research conducted by the Certified Financial Planner Board of Standards, people who work with a financial planner are more likely to have greater levels of savings and to be on schedule to accomplish their financial objectives.

Establishing a solid financial foundation requires time and work, but it is well worth the effort in the long run. Anyone may achieve financial success and build a brighter future by making a budget, saving for emergencies, paying off high-interest debt, investing for retirement, and getting expert counsel.
