Your shop's alarm system still matters. But a breach today isn't just about a broken window or missing inventory. It's about the customer list sitting on your server, the payroll data, the contracts you drafted last week. What happens when that data leaks?

Physical security still holds weight. But an internet security breach means losing vital customer information. It means outsiders could infiltrate your business data and use it for fraud.

You need antivirus software. If you don’t have it installed, take 30 minutes right now. Get it running. Even if you plan to research the best internet security suite for your business, download a free trial version. Protect your data and systems immediately.

Why the rush? Every business relies on the internet for client communication. Hackers know this. They release new viruses and Trojans daily, hoping to slip into an unprotected system.

While you investigate security suites, download a trial version for temporary cover. Kaspersky offers trials for all its products. This lets you test the software and see it work.

You’ll need separate antivirus software for Macs and Windows. If you use smartphones for business, they also need antivirus. Kaspersky mobile antivirus works across phone models and operating systems.

You need specific features in any internet security suite you buy. Demand these:

  • Keylogger protection. Keyloggers record your keystrokes. This lets hackers steal banking codes, credit card details, and other secure information.
  • Adware protection. Adware often installs with new software. It shows up as toolbars, popups, or shortcuts. Adware slows your computer and clogs your system. Protection blocks it.
  • Spyware protection. Spyware is adware's nastier cousin. It monitors your browsing habits and purchases. Spyware stores personal data, which can lead to identity theft, not just market research.
  • Rootkit protection. A rootkit is malware you don't want on your PC. It hides itself, often needing specialist software to remove. Sometimes, you must erase the hard drive and reinstall the operating system to get rid of it.
  • Removable media protection. Hackers' viruses don't just come from the internet. They can damage USB sticks or portable hard drives you plug into your PC. A good security suite scans removable media for threats.
  • Custom scans. You should choose which files or groups of files your antivirus scans, instead of running a full hard drive scan every time. Most antivirus subscriptions offer custom scans.
Most major internet security providers offer these features, and more. Review the details before you subscribe. Ask your business network which brands they trust and find easy to use.
